# Visual QA Checklist

This checklist protects the new Remotion project from drifting back into diagram-first output.

## Required Evidence

- Stills at the hook frame, event frame, proof birth frame, and residue frame.
- A contact sheet before any final render.
- The approved source claim table for every number in the reel.
- The approved styleframe or visual target for visually complex metaphor reels.
- For Family B/C reels, include chart/source proof stills and a CTA residue still.
- For Lottie usage, include a frame where the accent is visible and a note linking it to `public/lottie/manifest.json`.

## Visual Gates

- The no-label test passes: the physical event communicates the mechanism without relying on every label.
- The hero asset is memorable and active, not a decorative shape.
- The proof birth happens after the physical event that creates it.
- The proof remains attached to the impact, gate, break, stamp, or transformation.
- The residue frame preserves aftermath, not a generic recap.
- Lottie accents support motion texture only; they do not replace the hero metaphor.
- Family B/C reels use light editorial grammar unless a dark pattern interrupt is explicitly approved.
- Chart proof is faithful when it is source-critical; adapted charts require a written rationale.
- Logo/icon assets act as mechanism actors, not decorative badges.

## Stop Conditions

- The reel reads as a dashboard, template, chart demo, or animated slide.
- The number appears before the event that should create it.
- A source label floats as detached proof.
- The final frame is an ending screen instead of a memory image.
- Codex needs to invent the visual world from prose alone.
- A Lottie asset is used without license/source approval.
- A chart label carries the meaning while the chart behavior does nothing.
