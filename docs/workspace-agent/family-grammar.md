# Family A/B/C Grammar

Family B/C is the primary house benchmark. Family A is a motion-energy secondary reference.

## Family B: White-Background Chart Explainer

Use for chart proof, market thesis, support and resistance, moving averages, candlesticks, and source-backed comparisons.

Rules:

- The chart is the proof object, not wallpaper.
- Text is bold, sparse, and attached to chart events.
- Callouts are born from source points, lines, or bars.
- CTA inherits chart residue such as underline, marker, bracket, or trace.

## Family C: White-Background Logo / Infographic Explainer

Use for institutional mechanisms, payment rails, tokenization, AI infrastructure, company comparisons, custody, settlement, and flows between actors.

Rules:

- Logos and icons behave as mechanism actors.
- Routes, rails, documents, servers, phones, stamps, and vaults do something.
- Proof is attached to a route endpoint, document stamp, or actor behavior.
- White or warm-white editorial staging remains default.

## Family A: Motion-Energy Reference

Use only for motion verbs and asset energy: snap, impact, glow, route, latch, reveal, orbit, and handoff.

Family A is secondary. It must not become the default dark visual shell.
